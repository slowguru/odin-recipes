Pad Thai

Noddles of joy: Homemade Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a beloved Thai stir-fried noodle dish made with rice noodles tossed in a sweet, savory, and tangy sauce. It features tender chicken (or shrimp or tofu), scrambled egg, and crunchy bean sprouts, all quickly stir-fried in a hot pan. The dish is finished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh herbs like cilantro or green onions. The signature sauce combines tamarind paste, fish sauce, brown sugar, and a hint of chili for balanced flavor that’s tangy, salty, sweet, and just a little spicy. It's fast, full of texture, and customizable - perfect for weeknight cooking or impressing dinner guests with something bold and delicious.

  • Serves: 2-3
  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

For the sauce:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste (or 1.5 tbsp lime juice + 1.5 tbsp rice vinegar)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian version)
  • 1.5 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp chili flakes (adjust to taste)

For the noodles:

  • 6 oz (170g) flat rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small shallot, thinly sliced
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 6 oz (170g) shrimp, chicken, or tofu (choose your protein)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions

Prepare the noodles

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water for 20–30 minutes or until soft but still slightly firm. Drain and set aside.

    2. Make the sauce

  2. In a small bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and chili flakes. Adjust to your taste (balance of tangy, sweet, salty, spicy).

    3. Stir-fry

  3. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
  4. Add garlic and shallot, sauté for 30 seconds.
  5. Add your protein (shrimp, tofu, or chicken). Cook until just done.
  6. Push to one side and scramble the egg on the other side of the pan.
  7. Add noodles and pour sauce over. Toss to combine. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  8. Add bean sprouts and green onions. Stir-fry briefly.

Serve and garnish with purpose

  • Creating a nest: Using tongs or a large fork, twirl the noodles into a loose nest or mound in the center of the plate.
  • Crushed peanuts: Sprinkle generously over one side of the dish.
  • Lime wedge: Place it on the edge or corner of the plate for squeezing.
  • Fresh cilantro or Thai basil: Add a small sprig on top for a fresh, colorful touch.
  • Optional: Add a small pile of chili flakes and extra sugar on the side (as done in Thai street food).

Tips

  • Use tamarind concentrate if paste isn’t available, but reduce the amount since it’s stronger.
  • For vegetarian Pad Thai, use tofu and replace fish sauce with soy sauce.
  • Add a squeeze of fresh lime right before eating for the best flavor.

After cooking up a fresh, sizzling Pad Thai, the real joy comes in that first flavorful bite. The perfect balance of tangy tamarind sauce, salty fish sauce, and a hint of sweetness wraps around tender rice noodles, crunchy peanuts, and crisp bean sprouts. Each forkful bursts with vibrant textures and layers of flavor—from the soft shrimp or chicken to the fresh lime juice and fragrant cilantro sprinkled on top. Eating Pad Thai straight from your own kitchen feels like a little celebration of bold, authentic Thai flavors that awaken your taste buds and warm your soul. It’s a delicious reminder that sometimes the best meals are the ones you create yourself.
